The storm deposited significant snow across all five boroughs:

  • Manhattan: Washington Heights reached nearly 15 inches
  • Brooklyn: Widespread totals of 10-12 inches, with over a foot in Williamsburg and Flatbush
  • Queens: JFK Airport recorded 10.3 inches, LaGuardia just over 10 inches
  • Staten Island: 10-14 inches, some of the heaviest citywide
  • Bronx: Similar totals across the borough
Central Park's 11.4 inches was a daily record and the most snow since 2022.

üö® Impact & Response

City Response

  • DSNY deployed one of its largest head counts ever
  • 15,000 MTA workers maintained transit service
  • Snow-melting operations prepared for the first time since 2022
  • Code Blue protocols activated for homeless outreach

Tragic Losses

Mayor Zohran Mamdani confirmed at least 13 people died from exposure, most of whom were homeless. A 60-year-old retired NYPD sergeant died of a heart attack while shoveling snow outside his church.
