NYC January 2026 Snowstorm

The Biggest Snowfall in Nearly Five Years

Overview

From January 23-27, 2026, a massive winter storm system, unofficially named "Winter Storm Fern," swept across North America, bringing heavy snow and dangerous conditions to New York City. The storm delivered the city's heaviest snowfall since 2022, with some neighborhoods receiving up to 15 inches of snow.

Impact on the City

  • Transportation: Hundreds of flights canceled at JFK, LaGuardia, and Newark. MTA operated on reduced schedules with some express trains running locally.
  • Schools: Over 400,000 NYC public school students learned remotely on January 26th.
  • Emergency Response: Code Blue protocols activated to protect homeless New Yorkers. Warming centers opened in all five boroughs.
  • Snow Removal: 15,000 MTA workers and DSNY crews deployed the largest headcount in years for snow removal operations.
  • Casualties: Tragically, several weather-related deaths occurred, including individuals experiencing homelessness and a retired NYPD officer who suffered a heart attack while shoveling snow.

City Response

Mayor Zohran Mamdani praised city workers for their preparation and response to what was his administration's first major snowstorm. The Department of Sanitation deployed one of its largest crews ever, with workers pre-treating streets with brine and operating around the clock to keep roads passable.

"We prepared for this storm together, and we rode this storm out together," Mayor Mamdani stated. "While the worst is behind us, there is still more to be done as we clear the snow and restore our city."
