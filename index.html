❄️ January 2026 Winter Storm

Historic Snowstorm Hits New York & Northeast

Storm Overview

Storm Duration: January 23-27, 2026
Peak Impact: Sunday, January 25, 2026
Storm Nickname: Winter Storm Fern
Scale: Nearly 2,000 miles across North America

From January 23 to 27, 2026, a massive and expansive winter storm brought deadly and catastrophic ice and snow impacts across a vast stretch of land, from Northern Mexico to the Southern and Northeastern United States and into Canada. Meteorologists described the storm as "potentially historic" in its impact.

The system developed from an upper-level low on January 22 and steadily moved eastward across the Central United States. By January 25, it had transitioned into a powerful nor'easter as it reached the Northeast, bringing heavy snow and gusty winds to the region.

New York City experienced its heaviest snowfall in nearly five years. The storm began around 5:00 AM on Sunday, January 25, with near-blizzard conditions and snowfall rates consistently around 1-2 inches per hour throughout the day.

Transportation Disruptions

  • Airports: LaGuardia Airport closed at 1:00 PM on January 25. Over 11,000 flights were canceled on Sunday alone - the highest single-day cancellations since the pandemic.
  • Transit: NJ Transit and NYC Ferry suspended service. Metro-North operated on reduced schedules.
  • Schools: NYC public schools closed on Monday, January 26, with 500,000 students learning remotely.

Tragic Loss of Life

The storm proved deadly across the region. In New York alone, at least 11 weather-related fatalities were reported on January 25, with seven deaths occurring in New York City over the weekend. A retired NYPD sergeant, Roger McGovern, died of a heart attack while shoveling snow outside a church in Floral Park. In New Jersey, a 67-year-old man also died while shoveling snow in Verona.

Emergency Response

Government Actions

  • State of Emergency: Declared by New York Governor Kathy Hochul on January 23
  • NYC Sanitation: DSNY issued a Snow Alert beginning January 25 at 1:00 AM, deploying one of its largest crew counts ever
  • Code Blue: Enhanced protocols for homeless outreach remained in effect, with 24-hour outreach teams canvassing streets
  • Elections: Early voting for special elections in NYC was canceled for January 25

Mayor's Response

NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani, facing his administration's first major snowstorm, met with Department of Sanitation workers in Williamsburg, Brooklyn on Sunday morning. He was even spotted helping shovel out a car stuck in the snow, posting on social media: "As I have said, there is no task too big or no job too small. Happy to lend a hand and help out New Yorkers."

Snow Removal Operations

  • DSNY applied brine to highways, streets, and bike lanes starting Friday morning
  • Dozens of bike lane plows were deployed
  • BladeRunner 2.0 tracking system monitored operations from command center
  • Snow-melting operations prepared for the first time since 2022

Aftermath & Recovery

Extreme Cold Warning

⚠️ Dangerous Cold Event
Wind chills fell to -5°F to -9°F by early Wednesday morning after the storm. The NYC Office of Emergency Management warned: "Being outside will feel painful. Exposed skin can freeze in minutes."

Nationwide Impact

  • Death Toll: At least 42 deaths attributed to the storm nationwide
  • Power Outages: Over 500,000 customers without power, worst outages in Tennessee, Mississippi, and Louisiana
  • Economic Impact: Damages estimated in excess of $4 billion
  • States of Emergency: Declared in nearly two dozen states

Notable Incidents

  • Avalanche recorded in Duryea, Pennsylvania
  • Plane crash at Bangor International Airport, Maine (6 fatalities)
  • Ontario Provincial Police responded to around 100 car crashes over 24 hours
  • Three brothers died in Fannin County, Texas after falling into an icy pond

Recovery Timeline

Monday, January 26: NYC public schools held remote learning. Airports reopened with significant delays. Sanitation crews continued clearing streets.

Tuesday, January 27: Schools reopened. Transit services gradually restored to normal. Over 2,000 flights still canceled nationwide.

Ongoing: Sub-freezing temperatures expected to persist through early February, keeping snow on the ground for an extended period.

Historical Context

This storm represented New York City's heaviest snowfall since February 2021, making it the most significant winter weather event in nearly five years. The 11.4 inches recorded in Central Park set a daily snowfall record for January 25.

The storm's scale - encompassing nearly 2,000 miles at its peak - made it one of the most expansive winter weather systems in recent American history, affecting regions from Texas to Maine and stretching into Canada.
